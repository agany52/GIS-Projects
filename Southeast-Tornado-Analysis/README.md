# 2023 Tornado Events — Southeast & Mid-Atlantic US

**Tools:** QGIS 3.34, NOAA Storm Events Database  
**Data Source:** [NOAA Storm Events Database, 2023](https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/pub/data/swdi/stormevents/csvfiles/)

## Overview
Spatial analysis of 2023 tornado events across the Southeast and Mid-Atlantic United States, visualized by intensity using the Enhanced Fujita (EF) scale.

## Key Findings
- **Coastal pattern:** Tornado events cluster along the Atlantic coast, predominantly EF0-EF1, consistent with weak tornadoes spawned by landfalling tropical systems
- **Dixie Alley intensity gradient:** Higher intensity tornadoes (EF2-EF4) concentrate in the Alabama/Mississippi/Tennessee corridor, consistent with known Dixie Alley climatology
- **Appalachian gap:** A notable reduction in tornado activity over the Appalachian Mountains — partly terrain-driven (mountains disrupt low-level wind shear) and partly a reflection of population reporting bias in rural areas
- **Population bias:** Point clustering near urban centers reflects higher spotter and emergency management density, not necessarily higher tornado frequency
